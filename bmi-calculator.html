What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a screening measure that uses your height and weight to estimate whether you are at a healthy weight. It is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in metres.

BMI is a useful population-level screening tool, but it does not account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution. Athletes may have a high BMI while having low body fat. Always use BMI alongside other measurements and consult a healthcare provider for a full picture.

BMI ranges

  • Below 18.5 — Underweight
  • 18.5 to 24.9 — Normal weight
  • 25.0 to 29.9 — Overweight
  • 30.0 to 34.9 — Obese class I
  • 35.0 and above — Obese class II+

Limitations of BMI

BMI does not distinguish between muscle and fat. Highly muscular individuals often score in the overweight or obese range despite having low body fat.

For a more complete picture, use the Body Fat % Calculator alongside your BMI result.
