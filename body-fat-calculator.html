How is body fat % estimated?

This calculator uses the U.S. Navy circumference method, developed by Hodgdon and Beckett (1984). It uses measurements of your neck, waist, and hips (women only) alongside your height to estimate body fat percentage — no equipment beyond a soft tape measure required.

While not as precise as DEXA scanning or hydrostatic weighing, the Navy method is one of the most accurate tape-measure techniques available, with a typical margin of error of ±3–4%.

Body fat percentage is a more meaningful health marker than BMI alone, because it distinguishes between fat mass and lean mass. Two people with the same BMI can have very different body compositions.

Healthy body fat ranges

  • Men: Essential fat 2–5%, Athletic 6–13%, Fit 14–17%, Average 18–24%, Obese 25%+
  • Women: Essential fat 10–13%, Athletic 14–20%, Fit 21–24%, Average 25–31%, Obese 32%+
  • Women naturally carry more fat due to hormonal and reproductive needs
  • Essential fat is the minimum needed for basic physiological function

How to measure accurately

Use a soft tape measure pulled snug but not tight. Measure directly against skin, not over clothing. Take each measurement twice and average the results for greater accuracy.

Measure at the same time of day — ideally morning before eating — for consistent tracking over time.
