How does this calculator work?

This tool uses a simple but reliable model: one pound of body fat contains roughly 3,500 calories (7,700 kcal per kg). If you maintain a consistent daily calorie deficit, your body draws on stored fat to make up the difference.

Enter your current weight, goal weight, and how large a deficit you plan to maintain. The calculator divides the total calories to be burned by your daily deficit to estimate the number of days — and the exact date — you could reach your goal.

Real-world results vary. Metabolism adapts over time, adherence fluctuates, and water weight causes short-term swings. Use this timeline as a motivating target, not a guarantee.

Realistic expectations

  • 0.5 lb/week — very sustainable, minimal hunger
  • 1 lb/week — the most recommended rate
  • 1.5–2 lb/week — aggressive, hard to sustain
  • Above 2 lb/week — significant muscle loss risk
  • Expect slower progress after the first month

Why timelines slip

Your metabolism adapts as you lose weight — a lighter body burns fewer calories at rest. This is why weight loss often slows after the first few weeks even with the same deficit.

Recalculate your calorie target every 4–6 weeks using the Calorie Deficit Calculator to stay on track.
